Everyone starts with “no experience,” so don’t stress. A resume is just a way to show you’re responsible, reliable, and can learn. You don’t need formal jobs to do that.

Things you can include:

School projects (especially group ones or anything with deadlines)
Volunteering (community service, school events, church, etc.)
Clubs, sports, or leadership roles
Babysitting, mowing lawns, or any side hustles
Relevant coursework (computer classes, writing, math, etc.)
It's good to get into the practice of framing what you’ve done in terms of skills (ie., communication, teamwork, problem solving, responsibility). Employers hiring at 17 are looking for reliability and initiative, not 10 years of experience.

If you want more detailed examples, check out the wiki. It has guides and samples for students.

Don’t stress, everyone starts with a blank resume. The main thing is to show you’re reliable, motivated and have some useful skills, even if they didn’t come from a paid job. E.g. what you can add:

• Education: put your school name, expected graduation year, results if it’s good, and any honors or advanced classes.
• Experience from school or life: group projects, volunteering, clubs, sports, pet sitting, tutoring, mowing lawns… all of that counts and shows teamwork, reliability and initiative.
• Skills: anything you’ve actually used: Microsoft Office, other tools, cash handling, social media, lab techniques, customer service, etc.

You can also add a short 2–3 sentence summary at the top. For example:

High-school junior eager to start a part-time job in retail or food service. Active in student council and volunteer at local animal shelter. Known for reliability, positive attitude and willingness to learn.

Keep it one page and make it clean and easy to read. Employers hiring for entry-level roles already know you’re just starting out, they mainly want to see that you’re dependable and willing to learn.
